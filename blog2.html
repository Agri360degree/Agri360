Major Pests On Cotton


1.Cotton Jassid (Amrasca biguttula)
Order:- Hemiptera
Family:- Cicadelidae

Interesting fact
It is a subspecies of leafhopper (Amrasca devastans).
Destructive pest of American Cotton

Symptoms
Crop becomes weak due to loss of sap and injection of toxins
Cupping/downword rolling and yellowing of leaves due to toxins

2. Cotton whitefly(Bemisia tabaci)
Order:- Hemiptera
Family:- Aleyrodidae
Interesting fact
Pale yellow body which is dusted with a white waxy powder
highly polyphagous and known to have biotypes.
Symptoms
Both nymphs and adults harm the plant.
They suck the sap from the under surface of leaves.
loss of cell sap
growth of sooty mould on the honey dew excreted by the insects which interfered normal photosynthesis
transmit leaf curl virus disease of cotton, leaf curl disease of tobacco, vein curling disease of okra and leaf curl of sesame

Management
Alternative hosts of whitefly are brinjal, ladysfinger, tomato and tobacco. Therefore remove these type of hosts crops.
Use sorghum and maize as crop rotation.
Destroy alternate weeds like Solanum nigrum.
Do not use synthetic pyrethroids.
Imidacloprid 200 SL @ 75 g a.i. /ha or
Thiomethoxam @ 45 g a.i. /ha
Spray of Dimethoate @ 1 ml/liter of water
3. Pink boll worm (Pectinophora gossypiella)
Order:- Lepidoptera
Family:- Gelechidae
Interesting fact
Caterpillars are pink in colour but there are pink and white longitudinal bands are appear when we watch them closely.
Pink caterpillars are found inside flower buds, panicles and the bolls of cotton
Most destructive pest of cotton
Symptoms
Eggs are laid near the bud/boll. Caterpillar feed anther and style of flower buds. When it comes to boring, old bolls are prefered. Caterpillar comes out by making circular hole. then it falls down from the bolls to the litter and pupates there. If they remains in the flower, the flowers do not open and give rosette appearance. Yong bolls are weak so thy are shed after a few days but larger bolls remain on the plant. Another problem arises when the entry hole is closed and we cannot differentiate between a healthy and infested boll. Within damaged boll, the two adjoining seed are joining together by pink boll worm and double seeed formation occurs
Characteristic symptom is "Rosette flower"
Ccaterpillar cause exessive shedding of the fruiting bodies
About half of the shedding (dropping) may be due to the attack of pink boll worm
Management
Use Bt. cotton varieties
Destroy plant debris and alternative host
Deep ploughing (with furrow turning plough) done by the end of February

4. Spotted boll worm (Earias insulenalvitella)
Order:- Lepidoptera
Family:- Noctuidae
Interesting fact
Serious pest of cotton and okra
Main identification mark:- seires of longitudinally black spots,
with dull green color
having tiny stout bristles

Symptoms
Characteristic symptom is 'Flare square' (Flare-up)
Infected bolls open prematurely therefore produce poor lint

MCQ


Q.No.1. Rosetting of flowers and double seed formation is the symptoms of
Ans:- Pink Bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella)

Q.No.2. The pest causing flaring of squares in cotton
Ans:- Spotted Bollworm (Earias vitella)

Q.NO.3. The scientific name of American Bollworm
Ans:- Helicoverpa armigera

Q.No.4. The main symptom of American bollworm is
Ans:- Larger circular bore holes with faecal pellets

Q.No.5. The vector of leaf curl virus
Ans:- White Fly (Dysdercus koenigii)

Q.No.6. Which cotton pest causes hopper burn?
Ans:- Cotton Jassid (Amrasca bigutulla)

Q.No.7. Curling of leaf upwards and yellowing of terminal cotton shoots is a characteristics symptom of presence of
Ans:- Cotton Aphid

Q.No.8. The spotted boll worm is
Ans:- Earias vittella
