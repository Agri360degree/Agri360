Pest Of Vegetable




Pest on Family Solanaceae



1. Tomato fruits borer (Helicoverpa armigera)
Helicoverepa armigera is the major pest of tomato.

2. Brinjal borer (Leucinodes orbonalis)
Brinjal borer attacks terminal shoot and the growing points are killed.
The caterpillar may destroy 4-6 fruits.
They may bore flower buds and fruits also.

3. Jassids {Leaf hoppers} (Amrasca devastans)
Potato and Lady's finger are mainly attacked.
Both the adult and nymph cause damage.
They suck up the sap from plant leaves and tender parts.
They secrete a toxin which causes hopper burn in plants.
Their legs are modified for hopping just like locusts or grasshoppers.

4. Hadda beetle (Epilachna dodecastigme)
Adult and grub both are responsible for damage.
They feed upper surface of leaves of Brinjal and tomato.
Therefore, pupae are found fixed with plant leaves, stems and base of the plant.

Pest on Family Cucurbitaceae



1. Red pumpin beetle (Raphidopalpa sp)
Beetles are destructive to young plants.
They can bite cotyledons, foliage and floweres and make holes into them.
Grub and Adult both cause damage.
They are destructive to young plants.
Grubs bore the roots, undereground stems and some time fruits touching the soil.

2. Fruit fly (Dacus cucurbitae) Eggs are laid on the shoft fruits, a cavity is made by the sharp ovipositor and about a dozen white cylindrical eggs are laid, mostly in the evening time.
After 1-9 days, eggs hatch and maggots bore into the pulp forming galleries.
Then the attacked fruits decay.

3. Hadda beetle (Epilachna dodecastigme)
Adult and grub both are responsible for damage.
