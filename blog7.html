Pest Of Maize




1. Maize Stem Borer (Chilo partellus)
Order:- Lepidoptera
Family:- Pyralidae

Rreproduction and Life cycle
Eggs are laid near the midrib on both upper and underside of the leaf surface. Young larvae (caterpillars) feed the leaf whrol. Older larvae make tunnel into the stems by feeding and grow properly . Then they become pupa and remain in the stem. Then, adult emerge from the stem. Again they mate, lay eggs and damage the crop. Therefore, Fusarium spp can easily go through the opening created by the borer.

2. White Grub (Phyllophaga)
Order:- Coloeptera
Family:- Scarabaeidae

White grubs are polyphagus pests. It has wide host range. The adult feeds foliage of trees and shrubs. But larvae (white grubs) eat roots of the plants.

Lifecycle
It takes 2 to 3 years fo complete the life cycle. Eggs are laid on the fresh dung which hatches after about 18 days. Then this larvae feeds OM then moves to plant roots. Larvae hibernates in winter in the soil and comes out the the temperature rises.

Management
Use well decomposed farm yard manure.
Collect the beetle by jerking the host plant.
Use trap corop like marigold and sunflower and repellent plant as garlic.
Deep summer ploughing to expose the larvae which may eaten by birds.

3. Rice ear cutting caterpillar (Mythimna separata>)
Order:- Lepidoptera
Family:- Noctuidae

Also called 'Northern Armyworm' or 'Oriental Armyworm'. Pest start infestation after a heavy and continuous rainfall after a long drought.
Symptom
At first larvae eat tender leaves of the plants. Then it eat the tassel and whole plant. Damaged is localized in one part of the field.

Management
Flooding maize field
Use light trap to collect adult moths.
Use Cypermethrin @ 2ml/lt .
